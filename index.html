Facts About Penguins

Penguins can drink salt water.
They have a gland located above their eyes that filters out the salt and allows them to drink seawater.
Emperor penguins are the only species of penguin that breed during the Antarctic winter.
They endure temperatures as low as -40Â°C and winds of up to 200 km/h.
Penguins have an organ in their beaks called the supraorbital gland.
This organ allows them to excrete excess salt from their bodies.
Rockhopper penguins are known for their distinctive eyebrows.
These eyebrows help to protect their eyes from the sun and saltwater spray.
Some species of penguins engage in prostitution.
They exchange sexual favors for stones to build their nests. This behavior has been observed in Gentoo penguins, among others.
The little blue penguin is the smallest penguin species.
They are only about 16 inches tall and weigh just 2.2 pounds.
